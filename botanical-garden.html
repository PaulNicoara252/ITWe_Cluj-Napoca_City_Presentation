Alexandru Borza Botanical Garden

See Gallery

Overview

The Alexandru Borza Botanical Garden is a scientific and recreational treasure located near the city center. Spanning over 14 hectares, it features a diverse collection of plants from around the globe.

Highlights include the serene Japanese Garden with its traditional bridge and tea house, the Roman Garden with archaeological remains, and the large tropical greenhouses. It is a favorite spot for nature lovers, students, and photographers seeking a peaceful retreat.

Short History

The garden was founded in 1920 by Alexandru Borza, a renowned Romanian botanist, who transformed the former private gardens into a public scientific institution.

Over the decades, it has grown into a major center for botanical research and conservation, affiliated with Babeș-Bolyai University. Today, it combines scientific value with recreational beauty, hosting thousands of plant species.
