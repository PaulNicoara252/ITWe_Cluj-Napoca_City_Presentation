The Casino (Centre for Urban Culture)

See Gallery

Overview

Located in the heart of Central Park, the Casino is one of Cluj’s most elegant historical buildings. Built in an eclectic style with Viennese influences, the white palace with its large windows and terrace overlooks the Chios Lake.

Originally designed as an exclusive venue for the elite, it now functions as the Centre for Urban Culture, hosting art exhibitions, balls, conferences, and community events.

Short History

The building was constructed in 1897 based on the plans of the famous architect Lajos Pákey (who also designed the Chios pavilion and the Continental Hotel). Initially, it served as a casino and a summer restaurant for the city's aristocracy.

During the communist era, the building deteriorated, but in 2012, it underwent a massive restoration project funded by European funds. It was reopened as a public cultural center, regaining its former glory and becoming a jewel of the Central Park.
