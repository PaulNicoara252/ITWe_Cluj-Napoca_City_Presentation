Tailors' Tower (Bastionul Croitorilor)

See Gallery

Overview

The Tailors' Tower is one of the best-preserved defensive structures of medieval Cluj. Recently restored and redesigned as a cultural hub, the tower now hosts temporary exhibitions, small art events, screenings, and educational workshops.

Its interior blends original medieval stonework with modern architectural elements, making it a great spot for history lovers, photographers, and anyone exploring the old fortifications of the city.

Short History

The Tailors' Tower was built in the 15th century as part of Cluj’s fortified medieval walls. It was named after the Tailors’ Guild, which was responsible for maintaining and defending this section of the citadel.

In 1601, the tower became famous due to a dramatic explosion that destroyed part of its structure during a conflict involving the armies of Michael the Brave. The tower was later rebuilt and continued to serve as a defensive point until the city walls lost their military importance.

Today, after extensive restoration, it stands as one of the few surviving pieces of Cluj’s old fortress.
