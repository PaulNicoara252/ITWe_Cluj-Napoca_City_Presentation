Hoia Baciu Forest (The Haunted Forest)

See Gallery

Overview

Hoia-Baciu Forest, located near Cluj-Napoca, is one of Romania’s most mysterious and famous forests. Known for its paranormal legends, unusual tree formations, and strange lights, it attracts tourists, photographers, and adventure seekers from all over the world.

Despite its spooky reputation, the forest offers beautiful walking trails, open areas for exploration, and a unique atmosphere that blends natural beauty with mystery. It’s a short drive from Cluj, making it accessible for both day trips and night tours.

Schedule & Prices

  • Access Open 24/7 (Public Area)

*(Guided night tours are available through private agencies)*

Short History & Legends

The forest is named after a shepherd (Baciu) who supposedly disappeared here with his flock of 200 sheep. Hoia-Baciu is often called the “Bermuda Triangle of Transylvania.”

Visitors and locals have reported strange lights, unexplained disappearances, and malfunctioning electronics. The forest is famous for its twisted trees that grow in unusual shapes and for the "Poiana Rotundă" (The Round Clearing), a circular area where vegetation allegedly refuses to grow. It has been the subject of numerous scientific studies and documentaries.
