Pharmacy Museum (Casa Hintz)

See Gallery

Overview

The Pharmacy Museum is located in the historic Hintz House, formerly the city‚Äôs oldest pharmacy (first attested in 1573). The museum offers a fascinating journey through the history of medicine in Transylvania.

Visitors can explore reconstructed interiors of a traditional apothecary, including the sales room (officina), storage room (camera materialis), and the laboratory. The collection features over 7,000 items, from wooden and ceramic jars to old medical instruments and manuscripts.

Short History

The building hosted one of Cluj‚Äôs first pharmacies, operating continuously for centuries. The last private owners were the Hintz family, who managed it until nationalization in 1949.

In 1954, the space was transformed into a museum under the aegis of the National Museum of Transylvanian History. The layout preserves the structure of a traditional pharmacy, offering an immersive experience into how medicines were prepared and dispensed in the past.
