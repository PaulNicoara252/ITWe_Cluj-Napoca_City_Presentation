Dormition of the Theotokos Cathedral

See Gallery

Overview

The Dormition of the Theotokos Cathedral is one of the most iconic religious landmarks in Cluj-Napoca and the seat of the Orthodox Metropolitan of Cluj, Maramureș, and Sălaj.

With its massive central dome inspired by the Hagia Sophia and its richly decorated interior, the cathedral represents a blend of Neo-Byzantine architecture and traditional Romanian elements. Located right in the city center, in Avram Iancu Square, it is a peaceful spiritual space visited by worshippers, tourists, and architecture enthusiasts alike.

Short History

The cathedral was built between 1923 and 1933, shortly after Transylvania became part of Romania. Its construction was seen as a symbol of unity and spiritual rebirth.

The architectural style is Neo-Byzantine, featuring a massive central dome reaching 64 meters in height. Inside, the frescoes and iconostasis follow the Orthodox artistic tradition, while the exterior is decorated with stone carvings and arches characteristic of Romanian ecclesiastical architecture.

Over the decades, the cathedral has hosted major religious ceremonies, national commemorations, and cultural events, becoming a central landmark of the city.
