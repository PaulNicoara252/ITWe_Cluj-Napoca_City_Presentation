Matthias Corvinus House

See Gallery

Overview

Matthias Corvinus House is one of the oldest surviving buildings in Cluj-Napoca and a key landmark of the city's medieval heritage. Located in the heart of the old town, it is best known as the birthplace of Matthias Corvinus, King of Hungary and Croatia (1458–1490).

Today, it functions mainly as the headquarters of the University of Art and Design, but the exterior and small public areas maintain their historical charm. It is a must-see for visitors interested in Gothic architecture.

Schedule & Prices

Note: The building is not a classical museum, so public access to the interior is limited. You can usually enter the courtyard during university hours.

  • Monday – Friday 10:00 – 17:00
  • Saturday – Sunday Closed (courtyard may open for events)

Entry Fees:

  • Courtyard & Exhibitions Free

Short History

Built in the 15th century, originally as a Gothic-style residence, the house later served as an inn and a hospital. Its most important moment came in 1443, when Matthias Corvinus was born here.

Over the centuries, the building underwent several changes but still preserves its medieval Gothic windows and layout. A bronze plaque on the façade commemorates its significance, making it one of Cluj’s most photographed landmarks.
